## What this site is

Self Hosted Systems is a reference for engineers running serious self-hosted infrastructure — homelabs, early product infrastructure, or systems operated outside large enterprise environments.

The focus is on **operational discipline**: the practices that prevent outages, reduce the cost of the ones that happen anyway, and build confidence that systems will behave as expected.

## What this site is not

This site does not publish gear reviews, affiliate links, sponsored content, or vendor comparisons. It does not argue for or against cloud computing as a philosophy.

The goal is to be useful to someone operating a real system — not to generate traffic or sell anything.

## How this site is written

Every page attempts to follow a small set of rules:

- **Assumptions are stated** — "this assumes a single host" is more useful than leaving configuration context implicit
- **Failure modes are discussed** — a recommendation without its failure modes is a half-answer
- **Tradeoffs are explicit** — the chosen approach is not argued to be universally optimal; the tradeoffs are named
- **Baselines are realistic** — achievable for a serious homelab operator, not just theoretically correct
- **Boundaries are acknowledged** — when something is outside scope or expertise, that is noted

## Who writes this

This site is written from the perspective of an electronic/electrical and systems engineer with experience in operational environments beyond the homelab.

The writing reflects what has been seen to break, and why, across a range of system sizes and operating contexts. The goal is not perfection — it is fewer surprises.

## Scope

The site covers operational and architectural concerns that apply to self-hosted systems broadly — not specific software distributions, specific hardware platforms, or specific cloud vendors.

Where specific tools are mentioned (restic, BorgBackup, Proxmox, etc.), they are examples rather than endorsements.
